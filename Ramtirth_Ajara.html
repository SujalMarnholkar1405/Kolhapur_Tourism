Ramtirth Ajara Kolhapur

Ramtirth, located in the Ajara taluka of Kolhapur, is believed to be a site where Rama, Lakshmana, and Sita stayed during their 14-year exile (vanvas), and is known for the Ramtirth Waterfall, a natural waterfall on the banks of the Hiranyakeshi River. Here's a more detailed look: Location: Ajara Taluka, Kolhapur.

Significance: Legend says that Rama, Lakshmana, and Sita stayed here during their exile. Ramtirth Waterfall: A natural waterfall on the banks of the Hiranyakeshi River. Scenic Beauty: The waterfall and surrounding area are known for their beauty, especially during the monsoon season. Ajara Ghansal Tandul: Ajara is also known for its Ajara Ghansal Tandul rice. Rivers: The town is situated on the confluence of the Hirnyakeshi and Chitra rivers. Other attractions: Ajara is a historical place with a very beautiful waterfall
